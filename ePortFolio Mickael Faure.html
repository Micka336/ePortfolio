Engineering courses

    During my studies at the t² preparatory school, we were asked to complete a six-week internship. While researching, one laboratory was more original than the rest due to its focus: Cherchons pour voir, located in the IRIT at Paul Sabatier University, which centers its projects around the visually impaired.

    Among their main projects, I worked with JAVA on the Tangible Box , a printed shelf representing a specific space, such as the premises of the IJA (Institut des Jeunes Aveugles). When the user moves the piece to a special square (a room, a staircase, etc.), a sound associated with that square is played. This allows visually impaired people to use this device to learn how to find their way around certain places in an interactive way.

    My role in this project was to develop a simulator for researchers. This has improved their working conditions in several ways.

     On the practical side, researchers can now simply open the simulator and make changes to it, rather than having to find an available Tangible Box. This has optimized teamwork.

    On the other hand, from an environmental and economic perspective, this avoids creating new copies that would only be used for research, and therefore saving on the necessary materials. Obviously, users remain on the physical device and not on this simulator, which is only dedicated to researchers.
